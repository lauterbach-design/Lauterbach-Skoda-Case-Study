01 — Overview

An AI-native workspace for automotive design teams

Škoda's design teams were spending weeks on tasks that are fundamentally generative: pairing colour variants with environments, translating sketches into photorealistic renders, and verifying brand compliance on incoming concepts.

This project explores a unified AI design studio — four specialised tools embedded in a single platform — that compresses those weeks into minutes while keeping creative control firmly with the designer.

02 — Problem Space

Where design time goes to die

Four compounding inefficiencies were slowing creative velocity across Škoda's studio teams.

⏱

Manual matrix production

Generating a full environment × material image matrix required briefing photographers, booking studios, and waiting 2–3 weeks for renders. By then, the brief had often evolved.

🎨

Sketch-to-render bottleneck

Designers had strong ideas but limited ability to rapidly visualise them at production quality. The gap between napkin sketch and stakeholder-ready image was costly and demoralising.

🛡

Brand DNA drift

Incoming partner concepts and AI-generated images frequently violated Škoda's design language — wrong proportions, off-brand grilles, non-compliant colour palettes — but there was no scalable review process.

🔁

Semantic signal loss

After large generative runs, design teams had no systematic way to filter outputs. Promising concepts were buried under noise, and selection was entirely based on gut instinct.

03 — Solution

Four tools, one studio

Each tool solves one bottleneck precisely, without bloat. Designers move between them in a unified workspace — the same project, the same car, the same brand context throughout.

01 / 04
Matrix Lab

Define environments on one axis, material finishes on the other, and generate the full cross-product automatically. What once took a week-long studio shoot is done in minutes.

  • Multi-axis environment selection
  • Finish types: gloss, matte, metallic, pearl
  • Batch generation with consistent lighting
  • One-click download of the full matrix
02 / 04
Sketch Accelerator

Upload a rough sketch and dial a creativity temperature between Production Ready, Concept Evolution, and Future Vision. The AI adapts physics, proportions, and materials accordingly.

  • Creativity Temperature slider (20% → 90%)
  • Three preset fidelity modes
  • Parallel generation for comparison
  • Editable global context prompts
03 / 04
Semantic Loop

Feed in a batch of generated images and let an AI agent analyse them against a Semantic Confidence Score — filtering outputs by how closely they match the original design intent.

  • Adjustable strictness / leniency dial
  • Semantic confidence scoring per image
  • Iterative refinement loop
  • Drag-and-drop batch upload
04 / 04
Brand Guardian

The centrepiece. Upload any concept, sketch, or competitor reference and inject Škoda's DNA — design language elements, compliant colour palette, and HDRI environment — with a single action.

  • DNA Injectors: geometry, face, crystal lights
  • Compliance palette enforcement
  • Before/after slider comparison
  • Consistency Mode for batch runs
04 — Design Process

From brief to prototype

Week 01

Discovery & user research

Embedded with Škoda design, engineering, and brand teams across three studios. Ran contextual inquiry sessions and shadowed designers through their current workflows. Documented 47 distinct pain points across the creative pipeline, clustered into four critical bottlenecks.

Contextual Inquiry Journey Mapping Stakeholder Interviews
Week 02

Concept framing & AI capability mapping

Ran a two-day co-creation workshop with design leads to align on the biggest opportunities. Simultaneously audited available generative AI capabilities — Gemini Canvas, Imagen, and multimodal agents — mapping their outputs to validated user needs.

Opportunity Framing AI Capability Audit Design Sprint
Week 03–04

Figma prototype: information architecture & interactions

Designed the full workspace in Figma — navigation, task flows for all four tools, and the project dashboard with real Škoda model data (Enyaq, Elroq, Fabia, Scala). Defined the creativity temperature interaction model and the Brand Guardian DNA injection UX.

Figma High-Fidelity Prototype Interaction Design
Week 05–06

Gemini Canvas: live generative AI integration

Brought the Brand Guardian tool to life in Google Gemini Canvas, wiring up real generative AI. Built the DNA Injector pipeline, HDRI backplate selection, Consistency Mode toggle, and the before/after comparison slider with live Gemini API calls. Iterated on prompt engineering to achieve reliable brand-compliant outputs.

Gemini Canvas Prompt Engineering API Integration
Week 07

Validation & stakeholder demo

Tested with five senior designers and two brand leads. Measured task completion time against baseline (manual workflow). Presented live demo to Škoda leadership. Captured qualitative feedback on the creativity temperature model and the semantic confidence scoring UX.

07 — Reflections

What I'd do differently

The creativity temperature model resonated immediately with designers — but I underestimated how much time I'd spend calibrating the AI's interpretation of "Strict Adherence" vs "Production Ready." The UX label and the AI behaviour need to be co-designed from day one, not treated as separate concerns.

I'd also invest more in the Semantic Loop experience earlier. Filtering a large generative run is one of the highest-value, least-explored design spaces in AI tooling — and we only scratched the surface.

Key Learnings
LEARNING 01
AI parameters need UX design as much as interface elements do. "Creativity Temperature" is a UX invention, not an API parameter.
LEARNING 02
Brand compliance through AI is only as good as the specificity of your design language documentation. Vague guidelines produce vague enforcement.
LEARNING 03
Designers adopt AI tools fastest when the output is comparative — a before/after slider beats a standalone generated image every time.
